Mutual Protection Agreement

Standard Form
Sefirot Consulting LLC · Confidential

Effective Date:  |  Parties: Sefirot Consulting LLC, a New Jersey LLC, represented by William R. Sanford, Managing Partner (“Sefirot”) and the undersigned party (“Client”)

What this covers.

The parties are entering a professional engagement. “Engagement” means any discrete project, advisory relationship, audit, retainer, or other scope of work in which Sefirot provides services to Client, whether or not documented in a separate agreement. Each party will share things the other would not share publicly. This agreement governs what those things are, what each party may do with them, and what happens when either party crosses a line. It covers every engagement between Sefirot and Client — current, and prospective — regardless of whether a separate engagement agreement exists, and regardless of geography. The Effective Date is the date of the last signature below.

What Sefirot keeps confidential.

All non-public information Sefirot discloses — whether orally, in writing, electronically, by demonstration, or by observation — is confidential. This includes:

Operational: Proprietary frameworks, methodologies, and operational systems developed across years of client engagements. Compliance architecture, permitting strategy, and regulatory management systems. Site and venue intelligence: layouts, access protocols, staff contacts, incident history, and institutional memory. Vendor relationships: who Sefirot works with, what they charge, and what they’ll do that isn’t in the contract.

Technology: The existence, architecture, and deployment of any proprietary software, AI tools, scheduling systems, data platforms, or workflow automation Sefirot develops or deploys. How Sefirot’s systems work is Sefirot IP.

Audit and procurement: Pricing data, contract terms, and commercial practices compiled through Sefirot’s audit and procurement work — across any distributor, supplier, conglomerate, or national brand. Audit methodology: how Sefirot identifies transaction irregularities, conducts covert operational assessments, and evaluates staff performance patterns. Except in such cases when otherwise compelled by discretion, binding agreement, or contract.

Executive and investment: Cap tables, investment pools, investor identities, product roadmaps, M&A targets, stakeholder maps, and any material non-public information disclosed in connection with an executive or advisory engagement. Sefirot will not trade on this information. Client will not share it.

Personal and adverse: Personal disclosures made by Client personnel or principals. Adverse incidents: regulatory exposure, legal risk, internal misconduct. Back-channel intelligence: counterparty positioning, negotiation posture, leverage maps, disclosed intent. This is held in the strictest confidence and does not leave the engagement.

Network: The identity and relationship context of anyone Sefirot introduces or discloses. Research partnerships: algorithms, pre-publication findings, patent applications, model architecture, training data.

What Client keeps confidential.

All non-public information Client discloses is confidential. This includes: commercial arrangements and counterparty agreements; financial information and operating statements; internal strategy and product plans; personnel, contractor, and partner relationships; ownership and capital structure.

The rules.

Each party agrees: don’t share it (confidential information does not leave the engagement without written consent); don’t use it for anything else (it is for this engagement only); don’t replicate it (neither party will use the other’s information to build capacity that approximates what the other provides, or to train or position any third party to do so).

Disclosure at engagement.

At the commencement of each engagement, each party will disclose any known material conflicts, contractual limitations, or existing relationships that would reasonably affect the other party’s decisions to engage. Sefirot will identify the existence of any existing client relationship that presents a potential conflict. Client will disclose any existing contractual obligation that limits Client’s ability to receive, act on, or engage with Sefirot’s performance of services as contracted by the client. These disclosures, once made, constitute the complete conflict record for that engagement — except where the conflicting relationship or information was unknown to the disclosing party at the time of engagement, in which case disclosure is required promptly upon discovery. Neither party is obligated to make further conflict disclosures until or unless a new material conflict arises. These disclosures can be expected to be delivered without judgement or reprisal and are simply used to improve by folding discretely into the mental modeling that underlies execution of services contracted by the client.

Multi-client engagements.

Sefirot operates across overlapping industries and markets and may at any time maintain confidential relationships with other clients, including clients whose interests may conflict with Client’s. Client acknowledges this in advance and does not require Sefirot to disclose the identity of any other client or to seek approval before taking on any other engagement. If a material conflict arises, Sefirot will disclose its existence and the parties will determine together whether engagement can continue. Sefirot will not share Client’s confidential information with any other client, and will not use private or sensitive intelligence from one engagement to material advantage in another without explicit consent.

Equity.

This agreement does not create an equity relationship. Any joint venture or equity arrangement requires a separate written instrument. Entry into an equity arrangement does not expand either party’s license to use the other’s IP or methodologies. Any entity formed or enlarged in connection with Client during or after the engagement is bound by this agreement.

Engagement retainer and re-activation.

Each engagement requires a non-refundable engagement retainer credited toward the first engagement invoice. Re-activation fees apply when Client suspends, terminates, or allows an engagement to lapse and subsequently seeks to re-engage Sefirot. Retainer amounts and re-activation tiers are set in Sefirot’s then-current rate schedule; the applicable tier is determined at the time of re-engagement based on elapsed time since the last paid invoice.

Exclusions.

Information is not confidential if: the receiving party held it prior to disclosure and can prove this by written record; it became public through no action of the receiving party; it was independently developed without reference to the disclosing party’s information; or a court or regulator requires disclosure — in which case the receiving party gives prompt prior notice and cooperates to limit scope.

Intellectual property.

Nothing here transfers ownership of any IP. Sefirot’s frameworks, tools, software, methodologies, site maps, and documentation remain Sefirot’s property regardless of whether they were developed during a Client engagement. Client’s pre-existing IP, proprietary systems, and materials disclosed during the engagement remain Client’s property. Much of each party’s confidential information may constitute trade secrets under applicable federal and state law; this agreement is evidence of the reasonable steps taken to maintain that secrecy, and does not limit any statutory trade secret rights. Work product delivered to Client is licensed for the specific engagement it was made for, and for no other purpose.

Return of materials.

Upon termination of any engagement, or upon written request, each party will return or destroy the other’s confidential information and certify in writing that it has done so.

Remedies.

Breach causes irreparable harm not fully compensable by money. Either party may seek injunctive relief immediately, without posting bond, in addition to all other remedies. Each party retains all rights under applicable federal and state trade secret law. Where Sefirot’s confidential information constitutes a trade secret under the federal Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. § 1836) or applicable state law, Sefirot retains all rights and remedies available under those statutes — including, where the statutory conditions are met, ex parte relief; exemplary damages for willful misappropriation; and attorneys’ fees. Nothing in this agreement limits those statutory rights. Note: Individuals who disclose trade secrets in connection with a government investigation may have immunity under 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b); this agreement does not limit or supersede that immunity.

Dispute resolution — select one, initial before signing:

☐ Option A — Litigation: state or federal courts, Hudson County, New Jersey. Loser pays fees.
☐ Option B — Non-binding mediation: JAMS or AAA, within 60 days of written demand. Does not preclude injunctive relief.
☑ Option C — Binding arbitration [Sefirot default]: AAA Commercial Rules, Hudson County, New Jersey. Decision final and enforceable in any court of competent jurisdiction. Loser pays fees, arbitrator costs, and administrative costs. Emergency injunctive relief available pending arbitration.

Governing law.

New Jersey law governs this agreement. Where a court of another jurisdiction applies its own law regardless of this selection, each party reserves all rights under applicable federal and state law. Sefirot manages confidential information in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001 information security principles; this constitutes the “reasonable steps” required for trade secret protection under applicable law. For engagements conducted primarily within European Union member states, the parties will supplement this agreement with a written addendum addressing EU jurisdiction and, where personal data of EU-resident individuals is involved, GDPR compliance obligations.

Entire agreement.

This is the complete agreement on confidentiality between the parties. Amendment requires a signed written instrument. It operates alongside, not in place of, any engagement letter, invoice, rate schedule, or equity agreement.
